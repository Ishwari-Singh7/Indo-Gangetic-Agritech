
# Indo-Gangetic AgriTech

## Overview

Indo-Gangetic AgriTech is a student-led research and technology project focused on improving the profitability, sustainability, and productivity of small and medium farmers in the Indo-Gangetic Plain of India.

The project aims to study farming practices, landholding patterns, irrigation systems, crop choices, input costs, market access, and government support schemes to identify practical ways of increasing farmers' income.

Rather than promoting one-size-fits-all solutions, the project seeks to develop data-driven and region-specific recommendations based on local conditions such as soil type, rainfall, land size, water availability, and farming expenses.

## Vision

To create a simple and accessible digital platform that helps farmers make better decisions regarding crop planning, resource management, and income optimization.

## Objectives

* Research agricultural practices in the Indo-Gangetic Plain.
* Study challenges faced by small and fragmented landholders.
* Analyze crop profitability and farming costs.
* Explore opportunities in crop diversification, agroforestry, horticulture, and integrated farming.
* Compile relevant government schemes and support programs.
* Develop a farmer-friendly recommendation system based on real-world data.

## Current Status

This project is currently in the research and data collection phase.

Initial focus areas include:

* Ghazipur District, Uttar Pradesh
* Eastern Uttar Pradesh farming systems
* Wheat-rice cropping patterns
* Smallholder farm economics
* Farm management practices

## Long-Term Goal

To build a practical decision-support application that enables farmers to improve income, reduce risks, and make informed agricultural choices while preserving soil health and sustainability.
